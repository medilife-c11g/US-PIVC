## RoB 2 — Randomized Controlled Trials

### 1. Kleidon TM 2025 (EPIC Trial, JAMA Pediatrics)
| Domain | Judgement | Rationale |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| D1: Randomization | Low | Computer-generated 1:1, stratified by DIVA risk, concealed allocation |
| D2: Deviations from intended interventions | Some concerns | Open-label; 3/84 USG and 2/80 standard crossed over; ITT analysis used |
| D3: Missing outcome data | Low | 0 lost to follow-up, 100% analyzed |
| D4: Measurement of outcome | Low | Dwell time and failure objectively measured; research nurses entered data into REDCap daily |
| D5: Selection of reported result | Low | Pre-registered protocol (ACTRN12621000206820), SAP published |
| **Overall** | **Some concerns** | Open-label design with minor crossovers |

### 2. Bridey C 2018 (BMJ Open)
| Domain | Judgement | Rationale |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| D1: Randomization | Low | Sealed envelopes, no stratification mentioned but groups balanced |
| D2: Deviations from intended interventions | Some concerns | Open-label; assessors not blinded to allocation arm; 1 case lost in USG, 1 clinical deterioration in LM |
| D3: Missing outcome data | Low | 57/57 analyzed per group (1 case lost handled in sensitivity analysis) |
| D4: Measurement of outcome | Some concerns | Catheter removal reasons documented by non-blinded nurses; extravasation assessment subjective |
| D5: Selection of reported result | Low | Pre-registered (NCT02285712) |
| **Overall** | **Some concerns** | Open-label, non-blinded outcome assessment |

### 3. Varghese S 2025 (IJMEDPH)
| Domain | Judgement | Rationale |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| D1: Randomization | Low | Computer-generated sequence, sealed envelopes, concealed |
| D2: Deviations from intended interventions | Low | No crossovers reported; pain scores blinded |
| D3: Missing outcome data | Low | All 90 patients analyzed |
| D4: Measurement of outcome | Some concerns | Complications assessed at ~30min and ~2hrs only; very short follow-up; outcome assessors blinded to pain scores but not to technique |
| D5: Selection of reported result | Some concerns | No pre-registration mentioned; published in low-impact journal |
| **Overall** | **Some concerns** | Very short follow-up (2hrs); no pre-registration; low-impact journal with limited peer review |

### 4. Leroux S 2023 (J Emergency Medicine)
| Domain | Judgement | Rationale |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| D1: Randomization | Low | Computer-generated, sealed envelopes, registered (NCT04218643) |
| D2: Deviations from intended interventions | Some concerns | Open-label; 1 unsuccessful placement in standard group excluded; ED staff not blinded |
| D3: Missing outcome data | High | 30/106 (28%) USG and 29/116 (25%) standard lost to follow-up for postremoval analysis |
| D4: Measurement of outcome | Some concerns | Postinsertion failure determined from EHR by non-blinded investigators |
| D5: Selection of reported result | Low | Pre-registered (NCT04218643) |
| **Overall** | **High risk** | Significant attrition (>25%) for postremoval outcomes; underpowered (target 582, enrolled 223 due to COVID) |

## Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS) — Cohort Studies (max 9 stars)

### 5. Feinsmith SE 2021 (J Vascular Access)
| Category | Stars | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Selection (max 4) | ⭐⭐⭐ | Representative cohort (all PIVCs at NMH); exposed from USGPIV logbook; non-exposed from EMR (same source); outcome not present at start. -1: Selection into USGPIV based on DIVA criteria (selection bias). |
| Comparability (max 2) | ⭐ | Cox regression adjusted for age, sex, BMI, LOS, hospital unit, anatomic location, gauge. -1: No adjustment for disease severity or indication. |
| Outcome (max 3) | ⭐⭐ | IFR from EMR (objective); adequate follow-up (30 days). -1: 31% of PIVCs missing IFR documentation. |
| **Total** | **6/9** | |

### 6. Dachepally R 2023 (Pediatric Anesthesia)
| Category | Stars | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Selection (max 4) | ⭐⭐⭐ | PICU patients with DIVA; exposed/non-exposed from same unit; outcome not present at start. -1: Groups not concurrent (traditional → USG transition over time). |
| Comparability (max 2) | ⭐ | Adjusted for age only. -1: No adjustment for catheter length (major confounder), disease severity, or LOS. |
| Outcome (max 3) | ⭐⭐ | Chart review; adequate follow-up. -1: Non-blinded chart review. |
| **Total** | **6/9** | Major confounding: USG=long catheter vs traditional=short catheter |

### 7. Shokoohi H 2019 (Annals of Emergency Medicine)
| Category | Stars | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Selection (max 4) | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Nested within an RCT (randomized allocation); both groups from same ED; outcome not present at start. |
| Comparability (max 2) | ⭐⭐ | GEE model adjusted for age, sex, BMI, comorbidities, difficulty, catheter characteristics. |
| Outcome (max 3) | ⭐⭐⭐ | Blinded research assistants assessed survivorship daily; 72-hr follow-up adequate; low attrition. |
| **Total** | **9/9** | Highest quality cohort study; nested in RCT with blinded assessment |

### 8. Favot M 2019 (J Emergency Nursing)
| Category | Stars | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Selection (max 4) | ⭐⭐ | All USGPIV from nursing logbook vs all standard IV from safety incident reporting (different sources); outcome not present at start. -2: Non-representative denominator (USGPIV from logbook = 291 CT cases vs entire SIV population = 29,217 CT cases); historical comparison. |
| Comparability (max 2) | ⭐ | Compared BMI, age. -1: No adjustment for catheter gauge, vein depth, patient acuity, or indication. |
| Outcome (max 3) | ⭐⭐ | CE events from safety incident reporting system (objective); adequate study period (33 months). -1: Possible underreporting in SIV group (no active surveillance). |
| **Total** | **5/9** | Ecological comparison; different data sources for exposed vs unexposed |

### 9. Paladini A 2018 (BMJ Paediatrics Open)
| Category | Stars | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Selection (max 4) | ⭐⭐⭐ | Consecutive paediatric ED patients; DIVA → LC (study) vs successful SC (control); outcome not present at start. -1: Selection into LC group based on failed SC attempts (selection bias). |
| Comparability (max 2) | ⭐ | Groups compared on age and sex. -1: No adjustment for diagnosis, disease severity; confounded by catheter type (polyurethane LC 8cm vs Teflon SC <6cm). |
| Outcome (max 3) | ⭐⭐ | Daily assessment by ward nurses; adequate follow-up. -1: Non-blinded assessment. |
| **Total** | **6/9** | Major confounding: different catheter type, length, and material |

### 10. Saltarelli NA 2015 (Ann Emerg Med abstract)
| Category | Stars | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Selection (max 4) | ⭐⭐⭐ | All DIVA patients requiring USGPIV + traditional at same institution; from EMR; outcome not present at start. -1: Retrospective; selection into USGPIV based on clinical judgement. |
| Comparability (max 2) | ⭐ | Logistic regression performed. -1: Limited detail in abstract on covariates adjusted. |
| Outcome (max 3) | ⭐ | Nursing documentation review; adequate study period. -2: Abstract only — limited methodological detail; potential for incomplete documentation. |
| **Total** | **5/9** | Conference abstract only; limited detail available |
